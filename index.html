Explainable Energy Scheduling in Residential Home Energy Management Systems

§ 01

Abstract

This thesis investigates the explainability of Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) decisions in residential Home Energy Management Systems (HEMS), using Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP) as an interpretable reference model. Through post-hoc XAI methods including SHAP and Integrated Gradients, it evaluates explanation fidelity, stability, and user-relevant transparency across scenarios of increasing complexity — from single-EV baselines to multi-EV environments with stochastic uncertainty.

§ 02

Research Overview

Motivation

DRL-based HEMS systems offer strong performance but remain opaque. This lack of interpretability barriers adoption in safety-critical, customer-facing energy systems where trust matters.

Core Approach

XAI as the central lens — MILP as a transparent benchmark, DRL as the learning agent. Post-hoc analysis bridges the gap between performance and interpretability.

Scenarios

  • Baseline: single EV, PV & battery
  • Extended: multiple EVs + stochastic availability
  • Controlled PV & load forecast errors

Expected Outcomes

  • Systematic XAI analysis for DRL scheduling
  • MILP as reference for black-box explanation
  • Design recommendations for trustworthy HEMS
