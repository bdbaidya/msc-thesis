Overview

Background

This thesis addresses the lack of interpretability in Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) for Home Energy Management Systems (HEMS) by positioning Explainable AI (XAI) as the central research focus, using MILP as an interpretable reference model.

Key Objectives

  • Investigate XAI methods for DRL-based scheduling
  • Compare DRL transparency vs. MILP interpretability
  • Analyze explanation stability under complexity
  • Reveal policy strengths & limitations under uncertainty

Methodology

Simulation-based HEMS with PV, battery & EVs. MILP as deterministic benchmark; DRL as trained agent. Post-hoc XAI (SHAP / Integrated Gradients) applied and cross-evaluated.

Conducted by

Deutsche Telekom Chair of Communication Networks at TU Dresden in collaboration with Kiwigrid GmbH.
